Ten facts about Arsenal

Read time: 5 mins

Sp•rs are bad

  1. Founded in 1886: Arsenal Football Club was founded by a group of munitions workers in Woolwich, London, and was initially known as Dial Square.
  2. First Southern Club in the Football League: Arsenal was the first club from the south of England to join the Football League, in 1893.
  3. Longest Serving Manager: Arsène Wenger was Arsenal's manager for 22 years (1996–2018), the longest-serving manager in club history, and won three Premier League titles.
  4. Invincibles: In the 2003-04 season, Arsenal went unbeaten in the Premier League, earning the nickname “The Invincibles” — a feat unmatched in the modern era.
  5. 13 League Titles: Arsenal has won 13 top-flight league titles, with their most recent in 2003-04.
  6. Record FA Cup Wins: Arsenal holds the record for the most FA Cup wins, with 14 victories as of 2023.
  7. First London Club in the Champions League Final: Arsenal was the first London club to reach the UEFA Champions League final, doing so in 2006 (though they lost to Barcelona).
  8. Highbury Legacy: Arsenal's former stadium, Highbury, was their home from 1913 to 2006. It is considered one of the most iconic stadiums in football history.
  9. Emirates Stadium: Arsenal moved to the Emirates Stadium in 2006, a modern 60,000-seat stadium that remains their home today.
  10. Classy Tradition: Arsenal is known for their iconic red and white kit, which they have worn since 1933, thanks to then-manager Herbert Chapman.
